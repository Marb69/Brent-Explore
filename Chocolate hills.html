Chocolate Hills

The Chocolate Hills are a unique geological formation located in Bohol, Philippines, consisting of at least 1,260 to 1,776 conical mounds spread over an area of more than 50 square kilometers. These hills are primarily made of limestone and are covered with green grass that turns brown during the dry season, resembling chocolate, which is how they got their name. The hills vary in height from 30 to 50 meters and are considered one of the most famous tourist attractions in the region, having been declared a National Geological Monument.



How go to Chocolate Hills?




  • Fly to Bohol: Arrive at Tagbilaran Airport or Bohol-Panglao Airport.

  • Transport: Take a tricycle or taxi to Dao Integrated Bus Terminal in Tagbilaran City, then board a bus or van bound for Carmen or Sagbayan.

  • Private Transfers: For convenience, consider booking airport transfers or joining a tour with vehicle service.

  • Driving: If you prefer to drive, rent a car or scooter and follow the route from Panglao or Tagbilaran City to Carmen, which takes about 1.5 hours.

  • Final Steps: Once you arrive, you may need to park and take a tuk-tuk shuttle to the observation area.

    • Best time to travel in Chocolate Hills



    The best time to visit here is during the dry season, from January to May, so you can see the chocolate-colored hills. But if you prefer to see them in their greenest, you can visit during the monsoon or rainy season, which typically lasts from June to December.
