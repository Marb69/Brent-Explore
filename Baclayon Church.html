Baclayon Church: A Timeless Symbol of Faith and Heritage

Nestled in the historic town of Baclayon, just minutes from Tagbilaran City, Baclayon Church stands as one of the oldest stone churches in the Philippines. Officially known as La Purisima Concepcion de la Virgen Maria Parish Church, this centuries-old structure is a stunning example of Spanish colonial architecture, built in 1727 using coral stones held together by egg whites and lime.


Inside, visitors are greeted by a serene atmosphere filled with intricately carved altars, vintage religious icons, and hand-painted ceiling murals that whisper stories of devotion through the ages. Adjacent to the church is a small museum that houses precious liturgical artifacts, antique hymnals, and relics from the early Spanish era.


Despite suffering major damage during the 2013 earthquake, the church has been carefully restored, preserving its original charm and historical integrity. Today, Baclayon Church continues to be a vibrant place of worship and a must-visit cultural landmark for anyone exploring the rich heritage of Bohol.

From Tagbilaran City:



By Tricycle


  • Widely available around Tagbilaran.


  • Approx. fare: ₱100–150 (one way)


  • Travel time: 10–15 minutes

By Jeepney:


  • Look for jeepneys going to Baclayon or Albur.


  • Fare: Around ₱10–15


  • Ask the driver to drop you off at the church.

By Car or Motorcycle:


  • Follow the Tagbilaran East Road heading towards Baclayon.


  • Signage to the church is visible along the way.


  • Google Maps/Waze Pin: “Baclayon Church”



Things to Do at Baclayon Church

Admire the Historic Architecture


Take in the stunning Spanish colonial design, coral stone walls, and the 19th-century bell tower. Don’t miss the detailed facade carvings and arched windows that tell the story of centuries past.

Enter the Church and Reflect

Step inside and experience the peaceful ambiance. Marvel at the gold-gilded altars, ceiling murals, and centuries-old religious icons. It’s a quiet place for prayer, reflection, or simply appreciating heritage.

Visit the Church Museum

  • View religious relics, antique vestments, and Spanish-era hymnals.
  • See old images of saints and rare artifacts.
  • Entrance fee is minimal, and it’s worth it for a deeper dive into Bohol’s spiritual and cultural history.

Light a Candle and Say a Prayer


Candles are available near the altar area. Visitors often light one to offer prayers or intentions.



Join a Mass or Religious Event

If your visit falls on a Sunday or holy day, consider attending a Catholic Mass. The church is still active with regular services and special events, especially on the Feast of the Immaculate Conception (Dec 8).

Take Photos (Respectfully!)



Capture the beauty of the church and its surroundings. However, avoid flash and respect signage or areas marked as no-photo zones.

Stroll Around the Church Grounds



The peaceful surroundings offer a lovely view of the sea and nearby heritage homes. It’s a great spot for a quiet walk or some travel snapshots.

Buy Local Souvenirs (Optional)



Sometimes, vendors near the church sell religious items, handmade crafts, or local snacks. Great for souvenirs or pasalubong.

Best Time to Visit Baclayon Church

Ideal Season:




  • Dry Season (November to May) is the best time to visit.


  • December to February: Cooler and more comfortable for sightseeing.


  • March to May: Hot but great for clear skies and beach + church combo trips.

Best Days/Time of Day:


  • Morning (8 AM – 11 AM):

  • Fewer crowds

  • Beautiful soft lighting for photos

  • Cooler temperature

  • Late Afternoon (3 PM – 5 PM):

  • Warm golden light for stunning exterior shots

  • More peaceful atmosphere

Special Events to Consider:


  • Feast of the Immaculate Conception (December 8):


  • The church’s annual fiesta celebration. Expect religious processions, a festive atmosphere, and lots of visitors.
