Camugao Falls

Nestled deep within the forested hills of Balilihan, Bohol, Camugao Falls is a peaceful sanctuary for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Surrounded by towering cliffs and dense vegetation, this waterfall offers a truly immersive jungle experience.



How go to Camugao Falls




  • Drive or ride to Brgy. San Isidro in Balilihan.

  • From the parking area, a steep 10–15-minute downhill trek through a forested path leads to the falls.

  • The trail can be challenging, especially after rain, so sturdy shoes are a must.







    • Entrance Fee





  • Php 20per person (may vary slightly)

  • Local guides are sometimes available and appreciated.

    • Best time to travel in Camugao Falls



  • Dry season (November to May) is ideal for easier access and clearer waters.

  • Visit in the morning or late afternoon for softer lighting and a cooler hike.

    • Travel Tips





  • Wear hiking shoes with a good grip.

  • Bring drinking water and snacks, but take all trash with you.

  • A dry bag is handy for phones and valuables.

  • The area is relatively undeveloped, so don’t expect amenities—just raw, natural beauty.



    • Swim in the Refreshing Basin

    Dive into the cool, clear waters at the base of the falls—perfect for beating the heat and unwinding in nature.

    Capture the View

    Bring your camera or phone—Camugao Falls is a natural stunner, surrounded by cliffs and thick jungle. Every angle is Insta-worthy!

    Enjoy the Jungle Hike

    The short trek to the falls is an adventure on its own. Walk through lush greenery, hear the birds, and feel the thrill of discovering a hidden gem.
