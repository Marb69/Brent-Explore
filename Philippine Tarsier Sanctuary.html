Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary

The Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary is a protected forest managed by the Philippine Tarsier Foundation, Inc. (PTFI). It is dedicated to the conservation and study of one of the world’s smallest primates: the Philippine Tarsier (Carlito syrichta), a species endemic to the Philippines.

The sanctuary spans over 167 hectares of secondary forest, offering a semi-wild and natural environment where the tarsiers can live, breed, and be protected from habitat loss and human disturbances.

How to Get There



  • By Tricycle or Taxi:From Tagbilaran or Panglao

  • By Private Vehicle: Via Tagbilaran–Corella–Sikatuna–Loboc Road

  • Tours:Often included in countryside tour packages

Entrance Fee



  • Open Daily:8:00 AM – 4:00 PM


  • Adults: ₱150 (may vary slightly)


  • Children:Often discounted or free under a certain age


  • Includes:A short orientation, guided walk to the observation area, and access to the visitor center.






Why Visit?



  • See Tarsiers Up Close:Spot these tiny, wide-eyed creatures sleeping or quietly clinging to tree branches.

  • Learn from Local Guides:Guided tours explain tarsier habits, behavior, and conservation efforts.

  • Natural Habitat: Unlike commercial facilities, this sanctuary allows tarsiers to roam freely in their natural environment.

  • Photography Friendly:You can take pictures, but no flash allowed—tarsiers are nocturnal and easily stressed.

Travel Tips



  • Keep quiet and minimize movements to avoid disturbing the tarsiers.

  • Do not touch or use flash photography.

  • Visit during weekdays or early morning for fewer crowds.

  • Combine with a trip to Loboc River Cruise or Baclayon Church nearby.





Things to do in Danao Adventure Park

Observe Tarsiers in the Wild

Wander through a marked trail and spot tarsiers clinging to branches. They're usually sleeping or resting during the day, so silence and minimal movement are key. A guide will help point them out without disturbing them.

Learn from Local Guides

The sanctuary has trained guides who share fascinating facts about tarsier biology, behavior, and conservation efforts. It’s a great way to deepen your appreciation for these endangered animals.

Visit the Tarsier Information Center

Check out displays, photos, and research materials about tarsiers and other Philippine wildlife. It’s a small but informative exhibit area right by the entrance.

Take Photos (Respectfully)

Photography is allowed, but flash is strictly prohibited to protect the tarsiers’ sensitive eyes. Capture the moment—quietly and respectfully.

Enjoy the Natural Surroundings

The sanctuary is a serene, forested area perfect for a short walk and some peaceful time in nature. It’s a nice break from the busier tourist spots.

Grab a Souvenir

Support the sanctuary by buying tarsier-themed items or local products at the small gift shop near the entrance.
