Anda Beach

Nestled on the eastern coast of Bohol, Anda Beach is a secluded paradise that offers a tranquil escape from the crowds. With its remote location & limited tourist infrastructure,
Anda Beach remains relatively untouched, allowing visitors to experience the true essence of pristine natural beauty.
The beach stretches for kilometers, boasting powdery white sands that feel like silk underfoot. The azure waters teem with vibrant marine life, making it a haven for divers and snorkelers. Anda Beach is the perfect destination if you’re looking for a slice of paradise away from the usual tourist hotspots.

How to Get to Anda Beach?

Getting to Anda Beach requires a little effort, but trust us. It’s worth every bit of it. The easiest way to reach Anda Beach is by flying into Panglao Airport in Bohol. You can hire a private car or take a van to Anda from there. The journey takes around two hours,but the scenic drive through the lush countryside makes it a pleasant experience. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from Cebu to Tagbilaran and proceed to Anda by land. Once you arrive in Anda, you’ll be greeted by friendly locals who are more than happy to assist you in getting to your accommodation.

From Manila

From Manila you will need to fly to Tagbilaran (via Philippine Airlines or Cebu Pacific) and then make your way to Alona Beach from there.



Things to Do in Anda Beach

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the vibrant underwater world of Anda Beach by snorkeling or diving. The crystal-clear waters are home to an abundance of colorful coral reefs and marine species, including turtles, clownfish, and even whale sharks if you’re lucky.


  • Island Hopping: Take a boat tour and visit nearby islands such as Lamanoc Island and Cabagnow Cave Pool. Lamanoc Island is known for its ancient burial sites and mystical caves, while Cabagnow Cave Pool offers a refreshing swimming experience in an underground pool.



  • Beach Bumming: Spend lazy days lounging on the powdery white sands of Anda Beach. Its tranquil atmosphere and breathtaking views make it the perfect place to unwind and soak up the sun.


  • Trekking: Embark on a nature trek to explore the lush landscapes surrounding Anda Beach. Follow trails leading to waterfalls such as Can-umantad Falls or hike up hills for panoramic coastline views.


  • Local Cultural Immersion: Immerse yourself in the local culture by visiting traditional villages and interacting with the friendly locals. Learn about their traditional crafts, taste local delicacies, and witness traditional dances and music performances.

    • Best Time to Visit Anda Beach

    The best time to visit Anda Beach is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. During this time, you can expect sunny weather with minimal chances of rain, allowing you to enjoy the beach and outdoor activities fully. However, even during the rainy season (June to October), Anda Beach retains its beauty and charm, with fewer tourists.
