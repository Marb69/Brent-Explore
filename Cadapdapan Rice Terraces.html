Cadapdapan Rice terraces

Located in the eastern part of the island, the Cadapdapan Rice Terraces is a beautiful rice paddy field carved out of the mountainside. The rice terraces were built by the indigenous people of Candijay using traditional farming methods and techniques passed down from generation to generation.



How go to Cadapdapan rice Terraces




To visit Cadapdapan Rice Terraces, you can take a bus or van from Tagbilaran City to Candijay.
The journey takes about two hours. From Candijay town proper, you can hire a habal-habal
(motorcycle taxi) to take you to the terraces. The ride offers scenic views of the countryside
and a taste of the local way of life.








Things to do in Cadapdapan Rice terraces




Photography



The terraces offer endless opportunities for photography. Capture the stunning patterns of the rice fields, the hardworking farmers tending to their crops, and the panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Trekking



Take a leisurely trek through the terraces and immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the area. The trails are well-maintained and offer varying levels of difficulty, making them suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

Cultural Experience



Engage with the local community and learn about their traditional farming practices. The farmers are often happy to share their knowledge and stories, providing a deeper understanding of the cultural significance of the terraces.

















Visitor Information on Cadapdapan Rice Terraces

  • Entrance Fee:    A small fee is charged to support the maintenance of the terraces and the local community.

  • Opening Hours:    The terraces are open to visitors from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM daily.

  • Best Time to Visit:     The best time to visit is during the planting or harvesting seasons, from May to October, when the fields are lush and vibrant.

Best time to travel in Cadapdapan Rice Terraces



The best time to visit here is during the dry season, from January to May, so you can see the chocolate-colored hills. But if you prefer to see them in their greenest, you can visit during the monsoon or rainy season, which typically lasts from June to December.
