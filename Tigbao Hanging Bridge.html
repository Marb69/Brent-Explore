Tigbao Hanging Bridge

The Tigbao Hanging Bridge, also known as the Sipatan Hanging Bridge, is a notable tourist attraction located in the municipality of Sevilla, Bohol, Philippines. It spans the Loboc River and offers visitors a thrilling experience as they cross the bridge, which sways with each step due to its suspension design.

From Tagbilaran City (Bohol’s Capital)



  • By Car or Motorcycle: Approximately a 1-hour drive (29 km) via the Loay–Sevilla–Bilar Road.

  • By Tricycle: A convenient option if you're traveling solo or in a small group.

  • By Bus: Catch a bus from Dao Bus Terminal to Sevilla. From the town center, hire a tricycle to the bridge.

From Cebu City



  • By Fast Ferry: Take a ferry from Cebu City Pier to Tagbilaran City (approximately 1.5–2 hours). Then, follow the directions above from Tagbilaran City.

  • By Air: Fly from Mactan–Cebu International Airport to Panglao International Airport. From there, proceed as above.

Tips for Visiting



  • Entrance Fee: ₱10 per person.

  • Safety: The bridge sways as you walk, and the bamboo flooring can be slippery, especially when wet. Hold onto the railings and watch your step.

  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds and enjoy cooler temperatures.

best Month



January to May:These months offer sunny weather, ideal for sightseeing, crossing the bridge safely (bamboo can be slippery when wet), and enjoying scenic views.

Best Time of Day:



  • Early Morning (8:00–10:00 AM):Cooler temperatures, fewer tourists, and soft lighting for photography

  • Late Afternoon (4:00–5:30 PM):Still good lighting and often less crowded.

    • Avoid:



    Rainy Season (June to November):Rain can make the bridge slippery and muddy the paths, making the experience less enjoyable and potentially unsafe.
