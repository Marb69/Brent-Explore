Firefly Watching

Firefly watching is one of the most magical eco-tourism activities you can enjoy in Bohol! It typically involves a peaceful river cruise at night, where you can witness thousands of fireflies lighting up mangrove trees like Christmas lights.

How to go in Firefly Watching


Abatan River (Cortes or Maribojoc)



  • From Tagbilaran City: Take a tricycle or van-for-hire (about 20–30 minutes).

  • By Private Car: Head west from Tagbilaran via Tagbilaran North Road.

  • Tours: Many local tour operators offer packaged trips that include transport and a guided river cruise.

    • Loboc River





  • From Tagbilaran or Panglao: Travel time is around 45 minutes to 1 hour by car.

  • Public Transport: Ride a bus or jeepney heading to Carmen or Loboc and ask to be dropped off at the Loboc Tourism Center.

  • Tour Option: Join an organized night river cruise with hotel pick-up included.

    • Maribojoc Mangrove Forest



  • From Tagbilaran: Roughly 25 minutes by car or motorbike.

  • By Public Transport: Buses or jeepneys to Maribojoc, then a short walk or tricycle ride to the mangrove area.


    • Entrance & Tour Fees for Firefly Watching

    Abatan River (Cortes/Maribojoc)



  • Tour Fee: ₱600–₱800 per person (includes boat ride and guide)

  • Inclusions: Life vest, guided boat cruise, environmental fee

    • Loboc River



  • Tour Fee: ₱500–₱750 per person

  • Inclusions: Night river cruise, guide, and sometimes light snacks or drinks

  • Dinner Cruise Option: Some operators offer a combo firefly + dinner cruise (₱850–₱1,200)

    • Maribojoc Mangrove Forest



  • Paddle Boat Tour: Around ₱400–₱600 per person (depending on group size and duration)

  • Kayak Option: Available in some areas for a more intimate experience (₱300–₱500)

    • Tips for Firefly Watching



  • Go on a moonless or new moon night for the best visibility.

  • Avoid using flash photography or bright lights – it disturbs the fireflies.

  • Bring insect repellent (eco-friendly, of course).

  • Wear comfy clothes and be ready for a peaceful, nature-focused trip.

    • Things to Do During Firefly Watching in Bohol

    Enjoy a Peaceful River Cruise

    Glide along a quiet river under the stars while surrounded by nature. It’s a relaxing and almost meditative experience.

    Marvel at the Fireflies

    Watch in awe as thousands of fireflies light up the mangrove trees like living fairy lights—truly a magical sight!

    Learn About the Ecosystem

    Local guides often share fascinating facts about mangroves, fireflies, and the importance of protecting their natural habitat.

    Spot Bioluminescent Plankton (Loboc River)

    In some areas like Loboc, you might catch a glimpse of glowing plankton in the water—another rare natural wonder!

    Take in the Night Sounds of Nature

    Hear the gentle splash of water, chirping crickets, and rustling leaves—it’s nature’s lullaby.




    Capture the Moment (Mindfully)

    While taking photos with flash is discouraged, many tours allow you to take long-exposure shots or simply sit back and soak in the view.
